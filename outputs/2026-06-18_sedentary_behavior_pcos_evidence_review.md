### Original claim
Previous epidemiological studies have reported inconsistent associations between sedentary behavior and PCOS, with cohort studies more often supporting a positive relationship and case-control studies yielding mixed findings [ 参考文献]. Our clinical cohort adds observational support to this association, whereas cross-trait genetic analyses suggest that the link is not solely attributable to measured environmental or clinical confounding. ### Evidence from Zotero
- Moran 2013 and Tay 2020 support the idea that population-based cohort-derived evidence reports increased sitting time or lower physical activity in women with PCOS.
- Peters 2025 provides prospective accelerometer-based evidence that sedentary time in early adolescence associates with later hyperandrogenism-related traits, supporting a PCOS-related phenotype link.
- Wright 2004 provides an older case-control example where diet/physical activity differences alone did not explain PCOS-related obesity, supporting the “mixed findings” clause.
- Kazemi 2022 is the best synthesis paper for overall heterogeneity across observational lifestyle studies in PCOS.
- Dapas 2022 and Stamou 2024 support PCOS as genetically heterogeneous/polygenic but do not directly prove sedentary behavior–PCOS genetic correlation.
