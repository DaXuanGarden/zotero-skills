## 2. Recommended Manuscript Text
> Several genetic observations were consistent with a directional contribution of sedentary behavior, particularly leisure screen time (LST), to PCOS susceptibility. Large-scale GWAS of physical activity and sedentary behavior have shown that LST is heritable and genetically tractable, with multiple loci and biologically plausible enrichment patterns, while sex-stratified genetic analyses further reported that genetically predicted longer LST was associated with higher PCOS risk (OR = 3.05 per 1-h/day increase, 95% CI 1.65–5.65). In our analyses, LST showed broader polygenicity than PCOS and stronger conditional enrichment among PCOS-associated variants than the reverse comparison, supporting asymmetric genetic sharing. However, the role of adiposity should not be overstated as independent: obesity and PCOS share substantial genetic architecture, and published mediation MR suggests that BMI may contribute to the LST-PCOS association. Thus, the genetic liability to sedentary behavior may capture PCOS-relevant pathways that overlap with, but are not necessarily reducible to, adiposity. Causal interpretation remains cautious because residual horizontal pleiotropy, phenotype heterogeneity, instrument validity and ancestry-specific effects may still influence the estimates.

### Recommended revision
> Several genetic observations were consistent with a directional contribution of sedentary behavior, particularly LST, to PCOS susceptibility. Large-scale GWAS and sex-stratified genetic analyses support LST as a heritable behavioral trait and suggest that genetically predicted longer LST is associated with higher PCOS risk. In our analyses, LST showed broader polygenicity than PCOS and stronger conditional enrichment among PCOS-associated variants than the reverse comparison, supporting asymmetric genetic sharing. However, the evidence does not justify a strong BMI-independent claim for PCOS; obesity and PCOS share substantial genetic architecture, and published mediation MR suggests that BMI may contribute to the LST-PCOS association. Therefore, sedentary-behavior genetic liability may capture PCOS-relevant pathways that overlap with, but are not necessarily reducible to, adiposity. Causal interpretation remains cautious because residual pleiotropy, phenotype heterogeneity, instrument validity and ancestry-specific effects may influence the estimates.

### Why this wording is safer
The revised version preserves the manuscript's main interpretation while aligning it with the strongest external evidence. It avoids overclaiming BMI-independent causality and explicitly frames polygenicity/enrichment as study-specific results.
