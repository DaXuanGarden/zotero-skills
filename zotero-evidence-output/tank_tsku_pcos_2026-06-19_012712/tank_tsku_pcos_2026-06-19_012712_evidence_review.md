## 2. Recommended Manuscript Text
> Beyond MST1R, the tiered gene-prioritization strategy nominated TANK and TSKU as additional candidate effectors. TANK is a regulator of TRAF6/TLR-related NF-κB inflammatory signaling, and the TANK signal is biologically plausible in light of evidence linking NF-κB activation to skeletal-muscle insulin resistance and the recognized inflammatory/metabolic features of PCOS [Kawagoe 2009; Wang 2015; Zhang 2010; Tantiwong 2010; Nilsson 2018; Stener-Victorin 2024]. In our analysis, the TANK variant rs3769983 showed pleiotropic association with both traits, whereas genetically elevated TANK expression in skeletal muscle was associated with lower PCOS risk in both SMR and TWAS-JTI. TSKU encodes Tsukushi, a secreted small leucine-rich proteoglycan/hepatokine implicated in energy expenditure, insulin/metabolic traits, melanocortin-related energy-balance circuitry, and exercise endurance [Wang 2019; Wang 2021; Furuhashi 2022; Li 2021; Wang 2022]. The pleiotropic TSKU locus and convergent expression-based associations in cerebellar tissue therefore suggest, but do not establish, a possible link between sedentary liability and PCOS-relevant neuroendocrine and metabolic pathways. These findings should be interpreted as prioritization signals rather than proof of gene function.
