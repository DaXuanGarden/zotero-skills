> Previous epidemiological studies have reported heterogeneous associations between sedentary or physical-activity-related behaviors and PCOS: population-based and meta-analytic evidence generally indicates lower physical activity or higher sitting time among women with PCOS, whereas earlier case-control evidence suggested that lifestyle differences alone may not explain PCOS-related obesity (Moran et al., 2013; Tay et al., 2020; Kazemi et al., 2022; Wright et al., 2004).
