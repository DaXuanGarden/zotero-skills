## 2. Recommended Manuscript Text

> Within this broader multisystem framework, ovarian single-cell analyses helped define the local cellular context of shared genetic risk. To define the ovarian cellular context of these shared signals, we integrated GWAS summary statistics with human ovarian single-cell transcriptomic data using scPagwas, with scDRS as an independent sensitivity framework. Granulosa cells showed the strongest enrichment for shared LST-PCOS polygenic risk, followed by macrophages. This pattern is biologically plausible because single-cell and functional studies of PCOS implicate granulosa-cell inflammation, oxidative stress, steroidogenic disruption and ovarian microenvironmental remodeling, while immune-cell/macrophage activity has been linked to a proinflammatory ovarian milieu. Pathway analyses further indicated enrichment of ovarian steroidogenesis, cAMP signaling and insulin-related pathways in granulosa cells, whereas macrophages showed combined immune and reproductive signatures. Together, these observations support the possibility that metabolic and inflammatory signaling converge within the ovarian microenvironment to influence follicular development and endocrine function, but they should be interpreted as supportive rather than definitive evidence of cell-specific mechanism.
>
> International PCOS guidelines recommend healthy lifestyle, including physical activity and behavioral/weight management, as first-line care, and observational syntheses show that movement behaviors, including sedentary time and physical activity, are relevant in women with PCOS. By identifying a BMI-independent genetic association between sedentary liability and PCOS risk, our findings provide a biological rationale for targeting sedentary time beyond weight control alone. In this context, sedentary behavior may represent not only a behavioral correlate of PCOS but also a biologically informative exposure that warrants mechanistic and intervention studies, particularly among women with higher genetic susceptibility.

## 8. Integrated Writing Advice

### Original claim

The draft links shared LST-PCOS polygenic risk, ovarian single-cell prioritization, granulosa/macrophage contexts, steroidogenic/cAMP/insulin and immune signatures, lifestyle guidance, and the implication that sedentary behavior is biologically informative for PCOS.

### Evidence from Zotero

- Zhang et al. 2022 supports scDRS as a single-cell framework for disease-associated polygenic enrichment.
- Tay 2020, Kazemi 2022, and Moran 2013 support the relevance of physical activity and sedentary behavior in PCOS, but largely through observational or synthesized observational evidence.
- Local Zotero records for scPagwas were not adequate citation records; PubMed expansion corrected this by identifying Ma et al. 2023.

### Evidence from PubMed

- Ma et al. 2023 supports scPagwas as a method for trait-relevant cellular-context inference from GWAS and scRNA-seq.
- Teede 2023 and Cowan 2023 support guideline/lifestyle management framing.
- Zuo 2023, Luo 2024, Xie 2023, Yan 2023, and Siddiqui 2022 provide mechanistic and contextual evidence for granulosa-cell, immune/macrophage, and ovarian microenvironment relevance in PCOS.

### Recommended revision

Use the revised text in Section 2. The main changes are: (i) separate method citations from biological interpretation; (ii) describe granulosa/macrophage evidence as biological plausibility rather than proof; (iii) avoid overclaiming guideline wording around sedentary-time reduction; and (iv) make the BMI-independent sedentary genetic association explicitly a current-study result.

### Why this wording is safer

The evidence strongly supports the methods and clinical/lifestyle framing, and moderately supports the ovarian cell-type biological interpretation. However, the exact causal/genetic claim about sedentary liability and PCOS independent of BMI appears novel or at least not directly supported by prior literature identified here. Hedged wording will reduce reviewer risk.
