# Quick Use: MST1R, Sedentary Behavior, and PCOS

[Back to project summary](../PROJECT_SUMMARY.md)

## Use Status

- Status: Caution
- Best use: Candidate gene discussion
- Main risk: External evidence supports MST1R/RON biology but does not establish a direct MST1R-PCOS ovarian mechanism.

## Copy-ready Manuscript Text

Cross-trait meta-analyses identified 12 shared loci, with the strongest evidence mapping to the 3p21.31 region containing MST1R. Colocalization analysis further supported rs7615206 as the leading shared variant between LST and PCOS. MST1R encodes the receptor tyrosine kinase RON, the receptor for macrophage-stimulating protein, and experimental and review literature links MSP–RON signaling to regulation of macrophage inflammatory responses, tissue repair, downstream PI3K/AKT–MAPK signaling, and metabolic homeostasis (Wang et al., 2002; Wang and Hankey, 2013; Li et al., 2015; Huang et al., 2020; Sanjeev et al., 2023). PCOS has recognized reproductive, metabolic and cardiometabolic dimensions, and recent genetic and TWAS studies support gene-prioritization approaches for dissecting PCOS heterogeneity (Dapas and Dunaif, 2022; Lyle et al., 2023; Stamou et al., 2024; Guan et al., 2022). Movement-behavior data further indicate that sedentary time and physical activity composition are associated with cardiometabolic markers in women with PCOS (Pesonen et al., 2024). In our analysis, MST1R was consistently prioritized by MAGMA, TWAS-JTI and SMR, with tissue-specific signals in subcutaneous adipose tissue, cerebral cortex, nucleus accumbens, heart atrial appendage and thyroid. This cross-tissue pattern places MST1R within metabolic, neurobehavioral, cardiometabolic and endocrine contexts relevant to both sedentary behavior and PCOS. Thus, MST1R may represent a candidate pleiotropic node linking sedentary liability with PCOS-related systemic dysregulation. However, these analyses prioritize MST1R for functional follow-up rather than establishing its mechanistic role.

## Must-cite References

- Wang et al., 2002; Huang et al., 2020 — RON/MSP receptor identity and inflammatory signaling.
- Wang and Hankey, 2013; Li et al., 2015; Sanjeev et al., 2023 — tissue repair, metabolic homeostasis, and downstream signaling context.
- Dapas and Dunaif, 2022; Lyle et al., 2023; Stamou et al., 2024; Guan et al., 2022 — PCOS genetic, reproductive, metabolic, and cardiometabolic context.
- Pesonen et al., 2024 — movement-behavior relevance in women with PCOS.

## Claims to Avoid

- Do not state that MST1R is an established PCOS gene.
- Do not state that MST1R is proven to regulate ovarian function unless a direct paper is added.
- Do not present MAGMA/TWAS/SMR prioritization as proof of mechanism.
- Do not cite PCOS context papers as direct evidence for MST1R-specific ovarian biology.

## Safer Replacements

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| MST1R has been implicated in ovarian function. | PCOS has reproductive and cardiometabolic dimensions, while MST1R/RON biology supports inflammatory and metabolic plausibility. |
| MST1R is a pleiotropic mechanism linking LST and PCOS. | MST1R may represent a candidate pleiotropic node prioritized for functional follow-up. |
| Cross-tissue signals establish mechanism. | Cross-tissue signals place MST1R in biologically relevant contexts but do not establish mechanism. |

## Reviewer-risk Notes

- Highest-risk issue: overclaiming direct MST1R-PCOS or MST1R-ovary evidence.
- Keep “candidate,” “may,” “consistent with,” and “functional follow-up” language.
- Cite current study results for rs7615206, MAGMA, TWAS-JTI, and SMR prioritization.
