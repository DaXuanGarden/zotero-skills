Movement-behavior data further indicate that sedentary time and physical activity composition are associated with cardiometabolic markers in women with PCOS (Pesonen et al., 2024). MST1R encodes the receptor tyrosine kinase RON, which regulates inflammation, lipid metabolism and tissue repair, and has been implicated in ovarian function and cardiometabolic traits [references].

### Evidence from Zotero
- Zotero supports PCOS as a reproductive, metabolic and cardiometabolic disorder, and supports genetic/TWAS approaches for PCOS gene prioritization.
- Zotero supports movement-behavior relevance in PCOS, including associations between 24-hour movement composition, sedentary time replacement, and cardiometabolic markers.

### Evidence from PubMed
- PubMed supports MST1R/RON receptor identity and MSP–RON involvement in macrophage inflammatory responses.
- PubMed supports MSP/RON links to tissue repair and metabolic syndrome, especially lipid/glucose metabolic homeostasis.
- PubMed expansion did not retrieve direct evidence that MST1R is mechanistically established in PCOS or sedentary behavior.

### Recommended revision
> MST1R encodes the receptor tyrosine kinase RON, the receptor for macrophage-stimulating protein, and experimental and review literature links MSP–RON signaling to regulation of macrophage inflammatory responses, tissue repair, downstream PI3K/AKT–MAPK signaling, and metabolic homeostasis (Wang et al., 2002; Wang and Hankey, 2013; Li et al., 2015; Huang et al., 2020; Sanjeev et al., 2023).

### Why this wording is safer
This wording cites direct biology for MST1R/RON while avoiding an unsupported leap to ovarian function or PCOS-specific mechanism. PCOS and sedentary-behavior references can then be used to support the relevance of the broader systems context, not as proof that MST1R causes PCOS or sedentary behavior.
