## 1. Bottom Line

The proposed limitations paragraph is well supported, but several claims should be phrased cautiously because the available evidence mostly supports methodological plausibility rather than proving that demographic mismatch biased the present estimates. The strongest evidence supports four points: (1) leisure screen time (LST) and sedentary-behavior GWAS exposures come from broad, self-reported movement phenotypes, whereas PCOS GWAS datasets are female and often European-ancestry with reproductive-age or adult case definitions; (2) sedentary behavior is multidimensional and LST alone does not capture occupational sitting, total sedentary time, light activity, MVPA, and sleep; (3) European-ancestry restriction limits generalizability and motivates multi-ancestry replication; and (4) experimental and mechanistic studies are needed before translating genetic or association signals into individualized PCOS lifestyle strategies.

## 2. Recommended Manuscript Text

Several limitations should be considered. First, demographic differences between the LST and PCOS GWAS populations may introduce bias. The sedentary-behavior GWAS used broad self-reported movement traits, including LST and sedentary behavior at work, whereas PCOS genetic datasets are typically female and often focused on reproductive-age or European-ancestry samples; therefore, residual age-, sex-, and phenotype-related heterogeneity cannot be excluded (Wang et al., 2022; Dapas et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2025). Future studies should address this issue through sex- and age-stratified analyses, age adjustment, and other comparability-enhancing approaches where appropriate. Second, sedentary behavior is a multidimensional exposure spanning occupational, recreational, and broader 24-hour movement contexts; LST alone may not fully capture this complexity (Wang et al., 2022; Kazemi et al., 2022; Pesonen et al., 2024). Future work should incorporate broader and preferably objective measures of sedentary behavior to better characterize its relationship with PCOS. Third, the main genetic analyses were restricted to European-ancestry populations, limiting generalizability and underscoring the need for multi-ancestry replication (Dapas et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2025; Zhao et al., 2025). Finally, additional experimental and mechanistic work will be needed to move from association to mechanism and to determine whether these signals can inform individualized lifestyle strategies for PCOS (Wang et al., 2022; Taylor et al., 2021; Kazemi et al., 2022).

## 8. Integrated Writing Advice

- Use cautious causal language. The evidence supports plausible demographic and phenotype mismatch, not proof that bias occurred in the present study.
- Replace “raising the possibility of age- and sex-related confounding” with “therefore, residual age-, sex-, and phenotype-related heterogeneity cannot be excluded.” This is more defensible for GWAS/MR contexts.
- Do not overclaim LST as equivalent to all sedentary behavior. Wang et al. distinguish LST from sedentary behavior at work, and Pesonen et al. show that sedentary behavior should be interpreted within a 24-hour movement composition.
- If the manuscript keeps “propensity score matching,” add a statistical-methods citation outside this evidence package or rephrase as “comparability-enhancing approaches.”
- For individualized lifestyle strategies, link the argument to limited PCOS-specific lifestyle evidence (Kazemi et al.), experimental sitting-interruption evidence (Taylor et al.), and the need to understand mechanisms/mediation (Wang et al.).
