## Copy-ready Manuscript Text

Several limitations should be considered. First, demographic differences between the LST and PCOS GWAS populations may introduce bias. The sedentary-behavior GWAS used broad self-reported movement traits, including LST and sedentary behavior at work, whereas PCOS genetic datasets are typically female and often focused on reproductive-age or European-ancestry samples; therefore, residual age-, sex-, and phenotype-related heterogeneity cannot be excluded (Wang et al., 2022; Dapas et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2025). Future studies should address this issue through sex- and age-stratified analyses, age adjustment, and other comparability-enhancing approaches where appropriate. Second, sedentary behavior is a multidimensional exposure spanning occupational, recreational, and broader 24-hour movement contexts; LST alone may not fully capture this complexity (Wang et al., 2022; Kazemi et al., 2022; Pesonen et al., 2024). Future work should incorporate broader and preferably objective measures of sedentary behavior to better characterize its relationship with PCOS. Third, the main genetic analyses were restricted to European-ancestry populations, limiting generalizability and underscoring the need for multi-ancestry replication (Dapas et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2025; Zhao et al., 2025). Finally, additional experimental and mechanistic work will be needed to move from association to mechanism and to determine whether these signals can inform individualized lifestyle strategies for PCOS (Wang et al., 2022; Taylor et al., 2021; Kazemi et al., 2022).

## Must-cite References

- Wang et al., 2022 — LST/sedentary behavior GWAS exposure source and BMI mediation/confounding context.
- Dapas et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2025; Zhao et al., 2025 — PCOS demographic structure, European-ancestry limitation, and multi-ancestry replication.
- Kazemi et al., 2022; Pesonen et al., 2024; Taylor et al., 2021 — PCOS lifestyle, 24-hour movement composition, and experimental sitting-interruption context.

## Claims to Avoid

- Do not state that demographic mismatch definitively biased the present estimates.
- Do not equate LST with total sedentary behavior.
- Do not imply current evidence already supports individualized lifestyle prescriptions.
- Do not keep “propensity score matching” as a specific recommendation unless a dedicated methods citation is added.

## Safer Replacements

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Demographic differences introduced bias. | Demographic differences may introduce bias; residual heterogeneity cannot be excluded. |
| LST represents sedentary behavior. | LST captures one recreational component of a multidimensional sedentary-behavior exposure. |
| Propensity score matching should be used. | Comparability-enhancing approaches should be used where appropriate. |
