# Quick Use: Sedentary Behavior, LST GWAS, and PCOS Genetic Architecture

[Back to project summary](../PROJECT_SUMMARY.md)

## Use Status

- Status: Caution
- Best use: Discussion / genetic architecture framing
- Main risk: Direct horizontal pleiotropy or shared causal variants between sedentary behavior and PCOS are not established by the inspected evidence.

## Copy-ready Manuscript Text

Genome-wide studies suggest that sedentary behavior and PCOS may have partly convergent, but not yet formally demonstrated shared, genetic and biological architectures. A large multi-ancestry GWAS meta-analysis of physical-activity and sedentary-behavior traits identified 99 loci associated with moderate-to-vigorous physical activity, leisure screen time (LST), and/or sedentary behavior at work, with LST-associated loci enriched for genes whose skeletal-muscle expression changes after resistance training (Wang et al., 2022). Independently, PCOS GWAS and genomic reviews have identified multiple susceptibility loci implicating gonadotropin/neuroendocrine regulation, while recent multi-ancestry GWAS expanded the PCOS locus map to 94 independent loci (Hayes et al., 2015; Dapas and Dunaif, 2022; Zhao et al., 2025). Cross-trait and clustering analyses further link PCOS liability to adiposity, obesity/insulin-resistance, and metabolic pathways (Liu et al., 2022; Stamou et al., 2024). These findings suggest convergence on metabolic and neuroendocrine biology, but whether sedentary behavior and PCOS share causal variants through horizontal pleiotropy remains unresolved.

## Must-cite References

- Wang et al., 2022 — LST/sedentary-behavior GWAS and corrected 99-loci statement.
- Hayes et al., 2015; Dapas and Dunaif, 2022; Zhao et al., 2025 — PCOS genomic architecture.
- Liu et al., 2022; Stamou et al., 2024 — PCOS links to adiposity, obesity/insulin resistance, and metabolic pathways.

## Claims to Avoid

- Do not write that sedentary behavior and PCOS share causal variants as an established fact.
- Do not write that direct horizontal pleiotropy is proven.
- Do not use “88 LST loci” unless separately verified.
- Do not claim brain enrichment from this inspected evidence.

## Safer Replacements

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Sedentary behavior and PCOS share causal variants. | Sedentary behavior and PCOS may have partly convergent genetic and biological architectures. |
| Horizontal pleiotropy links LST and PCOS. | Whether shared causal variants or horizontal pleiotropy exist remains unresolved. |
| LST loci show skeletal muscle and brain enrichment. | LST-associated loci show skeletal-muscle enrichment; brain enrichment requires additional verification. |

## Reviewer-risk Notes

- Highest-risk issue: treating an unresolved knowledge gap as a demonstrated genetic mechanism.
- Keep sedentary-behavior GWAS evidence and PCOS GWAS evidence as parallel bodies of evidence.
- Use current-study analyses, not external citations, to support any claimed colocalization or pleiotropic locus result.
